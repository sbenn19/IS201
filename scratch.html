  • California
    1. California is home to the “Avocado Capital of the World.” Every year, Fallbrook holds an avocado festival to celebrate.

  • London
    1. London Underground was opened in 1863 for locomotive trains. In 1890, it became the world’s first metro system when electric trains began operating
    2. There are over 170 museums in London, including 11 national ones.
    3. The quintessential double-decker buses weren’t always red.
  • Grand Canyon
    1. In some places, the canyon itself is over a mile deep and ten miles across.
    2. Grand Canyon National Park is larger than the state of Rhodeisland.
    3. The drive from the North Rim Visitor Center to the South Rim Visitor Center of Grand Canyon National Park is about 200 miles (320km) and takes roughly four hours.
